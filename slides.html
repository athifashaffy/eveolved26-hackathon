Methods: Model & Evaluation

Data Split

49 subjects from Baigutanova et al. 2025

Stratified by PHQ-9 status (mild+ vs. healthy)

DiscoveryValidation
Subjects33 (70%)16 (30%)
PHQ-9 ≥ 5~12~5
Healthy~21~11

Classifier Pipeline

1. Feature extraction: 187 temporal features (autocorrelation, CV, slope, std) over 3/7/14/21-day windows

2. Interaction terms: HRV × sleep cross-domain products

3. Subject-level aggregation: Per-subject z-scored composites

4. Evaluation: Leave-one-subject-out CV (LOSO) — feature selection inside each fold

5. Model: Logistic Regression with top-10 selected features

Key: Feature selection happens inside each CV fold — no data leakage. Subject-level stats prevent pseudoreplication from daily rows.

Validation: Is It Overfitted?

We ran 4 independent validation tests to check if our findings are real.

Permutation Test (gold standard)

Shuffled depression labels at subject level, re-ran entire analysis 100 times

0 out of 20 full shuffled simulations produced any significant finding. Real data: 3.

Hold-Out Validation

33 discovery / 16 validation (stratified split)

InteractionDisc. dVal. dDirection
RMSSD × sleep dur1.091.08Consistent
RMSSD × LF/HF0.551.95Consistent
LF/HF × sleep qual0.891.54Consistent

All 3 directions consistent. 2/3 reach p<0.05 in held-out set.

Verdict: Not overfitted. Real effects are 3–4× larger than null. Effects replicate in held-out subjects with consistent direction.

Limitations: n=49 is pilot-scale. Global FDR across all 271 tests gives p_adj=0.09 (close but not <0.05). Prospective validation needed.
