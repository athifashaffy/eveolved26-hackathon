Depression is gradual — patients don't "fall into" it, they drift. By the time they report worsening, they've often dropped out.

But sleep and physiology destabilize before patients notice — and sleep is treatable.

The Insight: Destabilization Before Decline

Depression worsens gradually. But as physiological regulation weakens, the body shows measurable signs: sleep becomes irregular, HRV becomes erratic, recovery slows.

Known from physics

Systems under stress show rising variability and sluggish recovery — "critical slowing down"

Ecology Climate Finance Mood (PNAS 2014)

Our hypothesis

The same temporal patterns in wearable data can flag when a patient's regulation is destabilizing

Not a sudden tipping point — a gradual loss of physiological stability

Scheffer 2009; van de Leemput et al. 2014, PNAS

Five Novel Research Questions

Gap 1: HRV coefficient of variation (day-to-day variability) — shown as behavioral biomarker (2025) but never tested against PHQ-9
Gap 2: Critical slowing down in wearable HRV — proven in ecology/climate but never applied to depression physiology
Gap 3: Three-way temporal ordering (HRV x sleep x mood) — never modeled at the daily level
Gap 4: Digital allostatic load from wearables (low HRV + poor sleep + high resting HR) — never validated against PHQ-9
Gap 5: Autonomous biomarker discovery loop — doesn't exist yet

Statistical Rigor

Validation

  • Leave-one-subject-out CV (n=49)
  • No data leakage between subjects
  • Appropriate for small clinical samples

Metrics

  • AUC-ROC (discrimination)
  • Cohen's d (effect size)
  • Bootstrap 95% CI (uncertainty)
  • Permutation p-values (non-parametric)

Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across all 5,000 tests — no cherry-picking

Known limitations: n=49 is pilot-scale (hypothesis-generating, not confirmatory). PHQ-9 ≥ 5 threshold captures mild symptoms. Features are correlated across windows — we report independent feature families, not raw test counts. Baseline-only confounder control; daily variation unaddressed. Prospective validation needed.

Why This Matters Clinically

"The good thing with sleep problems is: if you treat them it hits two birds with one stone — a bothering problem that also triggers other symptoms."
— Prof. Dr. Steffen Moritz, UKE Hamburg

The Problem with Biomarkers

  • Predictive value of single biomarkers is never high
  • Depression is gradual, not a sudden episode
  • HRV/sleep are stress markers, not depression-specific

Our Answer: Monitor + Intervene

  • Detect sleep destabilization early from wearable patterns
  • Sleep is directly treatable (CBT-I, hygiene, medication)
  • Treating sleep improves depression, anxiety, stress — two birds, one stone

We don't claim to diagnose depression. We flag physiological destabilization — especially sleep — so clinicians can intervene before patients drop out.
